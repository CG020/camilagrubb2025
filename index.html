MDPI Motion Analysis Publication

Research paper: Around-Body Versus On-Body Motion Sensing: A Comparison of Efficacy Across a Range of Body Movements and Scales, published via MDPI under Bioengineering is a research publication on motion analysis utilizing on-body vs. around-body sensors to track mobility variability in patients.

On-body sensors (BioStamp) and around-body sensors (MOCA) were compared for efficacy of motion tracking and medical examination scenarios where motion abnormalities can now be analyzed quantitatively using the data captured rather than qualitatively as done so in many hospitals.

Sherlock3

A college coursework group project creating a question-answering system inspired by IBM’s Watson to tackle Jeopardy-style queries using Wikipedia data. Involves a robust indexing process, categorizing pages based on readability, and leveraging a custom tokenizer to enhance search accuracy. Querying employed a multi-layered approach using Boolean, MultiField, Term, and Phrase Queries to rank documents based on relevance.

Initial P@1 accuracy of 47% and an MRR of 0.52. Challenges like semantic understanding were addressed by integrating GPT-4 to refine rankings, increasing P@1 to 58%.

GreenBums

The app, GreenBums, was created to address the specific challenges of caring for desert plants in harsh environments. GreenBums aids users in developing a plant care routine through calendar warnings, a personalized plant catalog, weather condition alerts, and an in development image processesing scanner that detects wilting or unhealthy plants novice gardeners may have trouble identifying. GreenBums' audience involves seasoned plant hobbyists and new gardeners alike.
